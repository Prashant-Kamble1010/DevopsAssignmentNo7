📖 Answer
B2B (Business-to-Business): Transactions between two businesses. Example: IndiaMart — raw material suppliers sell to manufacturers. Large transaction size, long-term contracts, relationship-based marketing.

B2C (Business-to-Consumer): Business sells directly to end customers. Example: Amazon, Flipkart. High volume, smaller transactions, emotional/mass advertising.

C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer): Consumers sell to each other via a platform. Example: OLX, eBay — buy/sell used goods. Platform earns via listing fees or commissions. Value to users: buyers get cheap goods, sellers earn from unused items, platform provides trust/safety.

C2B (Consumer-to-Business): Individual offers product/service to a business. Example: Photographer selling stock photos to company on Shutterstock; freelancers on Upwork/Fiverr.

B2B vs B2C Comparison:
  • Customers: B2B = businesses | B2C = individual consumers
  • Transaction size: B2B = large/bulk | B2C = small/individual
  • Marketing: B2B = negotiation/relationship | B2C = mass ads, emotional appeal
  • Decision-making: B2B = slow, committee | B2C = fast, impulsive
Global Reach via E-commerce: A small artisan in Rajasthan can sell handicrafts worldwide via Amazon Global, Etsy. No need for physical export setup — just list online, ship via courier.

  3. App generates payment request or shows QR code

Advantages: Fast, cashless, spending tracker, offers/cashback, biometric security, accessible 24/7.
Risks: Phone loss = wallet loss, technical glitches during transactions, over-reliance on internet.

📖 Answer
E-payment Systems: Electronic methods of paying for goods/services without physical cash. Types: UPI, credit/debit cards, net banking, digital wallets (Paytm), digital tokens, cryptocurrency.

Digital Payment Requirements:
  • Stable Internet connection
  • Bank account linked to payment method
  • Registered mobile number for OTP authentication
  • Secure payment gateway on seller side (SSL certificate)
  • Device (smartphone/computer) with payment app installed
Credit Card: A card issued by a bank allowing user to borrow up to a credit limit for purchases, repaid later (monthly). Online: card number + expiry + CVV + OTP used for authentication. Provides purchase protection and chargeback rights.

Mobile Payments: Payments made via smartphone apps using UPI, NFC, QR codes, or wallets. Examples: Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm.

Digital Token-based Payment System: A system where a unique digital token (encrypted one-time code) replaces the actual card number for each transaction. Even if intercepted, the token is useless as it cannot be reused. Example: Apple Pay/Google Pay use tokenization — merchant never sees your actual card number. Steps: Card registered → token generated by bank → token sent to merchant → bank validates token → payment approved.

Benefits for Buyers:
  • No need to carry cash
  • Faster checkout experience
  • Easy tracking of all transactions
  • Reward points and cashback offers
  • Fraud protection and chargeback rights
  • Shop globally without currency conversion hassle
Benefits for Sellers:
  • Instant payment confirmation
  • Reduced cash handling costs and theft risk
  • Automatic bookkeeping and reconciliation
  • Accept payments from global customers
  • Reduced checkout abandonment vs cash-only
💡 Tokenization is a key concept — real card number replaced by token that is useless if stolen.

📖 Answer
Firewall: A security system (hardware or software) that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined rules. Acts as a barrier between trusted internal network and untrusted Internet.

Importance of Security: Without security, customer data (name, address, payment info) gets stolen → fraud → loss of trust → legal penalties → business closure. Security = customer confidence = business survival.

How Firewall Protects E-commerce (Role in System Integrity):
  • Blocks unauthorized access attempts to company servers
  • Filters malicious IP addresses and suspicious traffic patterns
  • Prevents DDoS attacks (flooding servers to crash the site)
  • Monitors data packets and stops SQL injection attacks
  • Creates detailed logs of all traffic for security auditing
  • Separates public-facing web servers from sensitive backend databases
Virus Protection Techniques:
  • Install and regularly update antivirus software (Kaspersky, Norton)
  • Schedule automatic system scans (daily/weekly)
  • Keep OS and all software patched and up-to-date
  • Never download files/software from unknown sources
  • Enable email spam and phishing filters
  • Use HTTPS and valid SSL certificates on all E-commerce pages
  • Train employees to recognize phishing emails
How E-commerce Website Ensures Safe Transactions: SSL/TLS encryption (HTTPS), two-factor authentication (OTP), tokenization of card data, PCI-DSS compliance, regular security audits, CAPTCHA to prevent bots, session timeout after inactivity.

Types of Threats:
  • Viruses/Malware: Software that damages systems or steals data silently
  • Phishing: Fake emails/websites that trick users into revealing passwords/OTPs
  • Intruders/Hackers: Unauthorized access to steal customer databases or payment info
  • Man-in-the-middle: Intercepting communication between buyer and seller
  • Ransomware: Encrypts business data and demands payment to unlock
Q24 — SPAM and Stock Fraud (Pump & Dump): Fraudsters send mass SPAM emails claiming a small company's stock will skyrocket (false news). Gullible investors buy → price rises → fraudsters sell their shares at profit → price crashes → investors lose money. This is illegal market manipulation.

Effectiveness of Firewalls + Antivirus: Together they provide layered security — firewall blocks external threats from entering, antivirus handles internal threats already in the system. However, zero-day exploits and social engineering (tricking people) can bypass both — human training and behavior is equally critical.

📖 Answer
Credit Card Online Transaction Flow (Q1 & Q5):
  1. Customer enters card number, expiry date, CVV on checkout page (SSL encrypted)
  2. Data sent to merchant's payment gateway (e.g., Razorpay)
  3. Gateway forwards to card network (Visa/Mastercard/RuPay)
  4. Card network checks with issuing bank — verifies available credit limit
  5. Bank sends OTP to registered mobile for 2-Factor Authentication
  6. Customer enters OTP → bank approves/declines
  7. Confirmation sent back through gateway → merchant → customer
  8. Settlement happens within 1-2 business days to merchant's account
Mobile Payments in Everyday Transactions (Q15):
  • Grocery store: Scan QR code at checkout → pay via GPay
  • Auto rickshaw: Show QR code → driver scans → payment received
  • Online food: Open Swiggy → select food → pay via UPI in one click
  • Utility bills: Electricity/water bills paid via Paytm without visiting office
  • Movie tickets: BookMyShow app → UPI payment → e-ticket on phone
Digital Token in Secure Payments (Q18):
  1. User registers credit card in Google Pay app
  2. Bank generates a unique token (e.g., 4815 XXXX XXXX 3271) to represent the real card
  3. Token stored on phone; real card number never on device
  4. At payment: token sent to merchant instead of real card number
  5. Merchant sends token to bank; bank validates → approves payment
  6. If merchant gets hacked: only useless token is stolen, not real card data

📖 Answer
Market Segmentation: Dividing a broad market into smaller, distinct groups of customers sharing similar needs, characteristics, or behaviors — enabling targeted marketing.

4 Types of Segmentation:
  • Demographic: Age, gender, income, education (e.g., luxury brands target high-income group)
  • Geographic: City, region, climate (e.g., winter clothing ads only in North India)
  • Psychographic: Lifestyle, values, personality (e.g., eco-friendly products for environmentally conscious buyers)
  • Behavioral: Purchase history, brand loyalty, usage rate (e.g., Amazon recommends based on past purchases)
Online Marketing Research: Systematic process of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting digital data about customers, competitors, and markets to support marketing decisions.

How It's Conducted (Q5 & Q19):
  • Google Analytics — track website visitor behavior, traffic sources, bounce rates
  • Online surveys — Google Forms, SurveyMonkey sent via email/social media
  • Social media listening — monitor brand mentions, hashtags, competitor comments
  • A/B testing — test two versions of ad/webpage to see which performs better
  • SEMrush/SimilarWeb — competitor analysis, keyword research
  • Customer feedback forms and Net Promoter Score (NPS) surveys
Role of Data Analytics (Q21): Analytics tools (Google Analytics, Mixpanel) process large volumes of data to reveal: which products are viewed most, where customers abandon during checkout, what time they're most active, which ads convert best. Enables data-driven decisions vs guesswork.

Segmentation Improving Marketing Efficiency (Q22): Instead of spending ₹1 lakh on mass ads, a company spends ₹20,000 targeting exactly the right segment → higher click-through rate, higher conversion rate, lower cost per acquisition, better overall ROI. Nykaa targets beauty-conscious women 18-35 — not everyone. Precision = efficiency.

E-commerce Marketing Strategies (Q4): SEO, PPC/Google Ads, social media marketing, email marketing, affiliate marketing, influencer marketing, content marketing, WhatsApp marketing.

Role in Customer Engagement (Q13): Personalized emails — birthday discounts, abandoned cart reminders, "back in stock" alerts, new arrival newsletters — keep customers connected to the brand between purchases. Creates ongoing relationship, not just one-time transaction.

Creating an Email Campaign (Q18):
  1. Define campaign goal (new product launch / re-engage inactive customers)
  2. Build/import email list (with opt-in consent)
  3. Design template using Mailchimp, Brevo, or Sendinblue
  4. Write compelling subject line ("Your cart is waiting 🛒") and clear CTA button
  5. Segment list — send different emails to new vs returning customers
  6. Schedule send time (Tuesday/Thursday 10 AM = highest open rates)
  7. Track metrics: open rate, click rate, unsubscribe rate, conversion rate
Email Marketing for Retention (Q3): Email marketing has 4x higher ROI than social media for retention because: direct inbox delivery (no algorithm), highly personalizable, can automate lifecycle messages (welcome → nurture → win-back sequence), measurable.
